National Parks

The National Park Service was created on August 25, 1916 by President Woodrow Wilson. The Executive Order in 1933 moved national monuments and site to the National Park Service. Now, additions are made through Congress which is the only way a National Park can be created. The National Park System covers more than 84 million acres in 50 states.
